We have been subjected to false advertising. 

I keep hearing, and believed for a long time that "C is close to the hardware".

That's not actually true.

K&R C was invented for rewriting Unix from assembler to version 4 for a PDP-7.

The PDP-7 has pre and post increment and decrement instructions.

C is "close to the hardware" for PDP-11 type machines, but, it is _not_ "close to the hardware" for other kinds of machines (say, the MC6800 and its descendants).

I don't think that we've sufficiently explored what is possible with cheaper, non-PDP-11 architectures. Our over-emphasis on C style programming (which is just a nascent form of functional programming) has blinded us to other possibilities.
