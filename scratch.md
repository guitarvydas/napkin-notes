One of the points swirling around in the back of my mind is that most of our current PLs are perched atop of operating systems. If we simply side-step the issue of counting LOC (Lines Of Code) in micro-code, and only include the LOC for operating systems perched atop of micro-code, my gut says that we get code bloat right out of the starting gate. Linux 40 million LOC, Windows (proprietary) est. 50 million-80 million LOC, MacOS (Darwin base) 80 million -86 million LOC. (And, yes, we _can_ create PLs that don't require current-breed-OSs - micro-python, sector lisp, forth, pbp/0D, (Erlang?, Java?) - and we *can* create programs that don't need operating systems at all - programs like Sketchpad, 20th century cartridge based games, pre-Windows 3.11 apps(?), Apple Postscript printers). Our current model of programming is well-suited to HPC, but not-so-well-suited to other kinds of things. IMO, further stretching the current model to fit other problem domains is like building epicycles.
