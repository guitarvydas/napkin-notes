- concurrency
	- locks, semaphores, etc.
- callback hell
- sequencing
	- requires `.then`
- async
	- async was stripped out by sync approach
	- I don't believe that it is possible to put async back on top of a sync substrate

I favour the use of multiple notations - e.g. small languages that need not be Turing complete. A notation custom-designed to support a given paradigm should not be stretched out of its sweet spot.

For example, Lisp 1.5 created a substrate for "functional programming". We've been stretching it out of its sweet spot for decades and we keep discovering "gotchas" and fix them in an ad-hoc manner. E.g. The Mars Pathfinder disaster led to the bandaid called "priority inheritance". Callback hell was tamed by [see 2026-04-25].

This leads to two possible conclusions:
1. it's OK to keep applying band-aids to our programming languages as we discover them
2. we need to ask the question: how can you tell when a notation is being stretched out of its sweet spot? [see 2026-04-25].
