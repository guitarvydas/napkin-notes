**The strongest ideas:**

**Things that need tightening:**

1. **"Python, JS, Haskell, Rust are all basically the same"** — this will stop readers cold, especially Haskell people. You're making a precise claim (they all enforce sequential/synchronous execution at the paradigm level) but it reads like provocation without the precision. What exactly do they share that makes them "the same"? You need one sentence that nails it before making the claim.
    
2. **"Functional paradigm" is doing double duty.** You're using it to mean both _mathematical/declarative_ and _synchronous/sequential_. These aren't identical. Prolog is declarative but not sequential. You might want a cleaner term — maybe "synchronous notation" or "sequential notation" — and reserve "functional" for when you mean specifically the mathematical function model.
    
3. **The BFT reference** — you have `[what is the reference?]` still in the text. Is it Lamport's Byzantine Generals (1982), or Castro & Liskov's PBFT (1999)? Also: is Bitcoin really a "Turing Award concept"? Satoshi is anonymous, and the Turing Award hasn't gone to Bitcoin. Worth double-checking that framing.
    
4. **"5-year-olds get parallelism (piano lessons)"** — the intuition is right but the example is thin as written. What specifically does a piano student understand that a thread-library programmer doesn't?
