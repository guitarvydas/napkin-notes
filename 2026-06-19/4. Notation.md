Notation and expressibility are important. The fact that something _can_ be represented in text form in a synchronous, sequential programming language doesn't necessarily mean that it _should_ be expressed that way. Back in the 1960s, text was about the only thing that machines could handle. Shoving ideas through the synchronous, sequential paradigm makes certain things impossible (like thinking about async parallelism - the best you can do is create individual reactors and then plumb them together, akin to using assembler). We can do better. Sticking with an inappropriate notation is like what was done before Galileo's time - i.e. adding epicycles to Ptolemaic cosmology. Additionally, Nobel laureate Ilya Prigogene wrote that the use of functional notation has set physics back by a century.
