Why don't I add XXX to emacs?

Reluctance to pile even more random stuff into emacs. I'm working on lots of other stuff and don't want to go down this rabbit hole at this time. Actually, I think that emacs (all code editors) should be chunked differently in the 21st century in a way that seemed too inefficient in the 20th century. Something like message passing between operations spawned off to daemon processes. Not more functions wired into the guts of the editor (because functions mapped to hardware bring along too much tight-coupling baggage). If I begin to go down this path, I will feel inclined to turn this into a mega-project trying to strip down emacs and fix the problem "right". The reference to emacs was really more of a prod to begin thinking about parsing as something that can be used for more than just for compiler-writing (esp. with PEG and Ohm). The T2T and PBP stuff I've got is keeping me happy.
