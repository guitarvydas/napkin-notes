From my perspective, DDJ was instrumental in popularizing C. There was no such thing as "open source". To see the source code to UNIX and C, you had to be a student at a university with a source license or work for a company that could afford a commercial license ($20,000 in 1980, equivalent to approximately $80,000 today). DDJ's SmallC (Ron Cain, 1980) was still copyrighted and not open source, but you could buy the magazine for $1.50 (and type the code in yourself, as I did). UNIX was originally written in assembler, then V4 was written in K&R C. Looking back, I conclude that K&R C was the beginning of bloat, ANSI C was a major escalation in the culture of bloat (ANSI C and K&R C are two very different languages with different purposes). BTW, DDJ's Tiny Basic (~1976) was the first time I saw a VM (virtual machine).
