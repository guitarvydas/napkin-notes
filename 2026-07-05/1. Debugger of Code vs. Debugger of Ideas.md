aside: It has become my opinion that "debugger" in the days of REPLs (lisp, smalltalk, forth, etc) meant something more than just "debugger of code". REPLs were used to debug _ideas_ (I've been using words like Software Architecture and Design). My perspective comes from the bias of thinking that "code" is just a building material like, say a piece of wood. You can build things with wood or you can merely concentrate on how to use (hand saw vs. power saw) and treat the wood. We've sloughed off the concept of designing architectures by developing tools like Continuous Deployment (the CD part of CI/CD) and waiting for paying customers to (very slowly) debug and give us feedback about our concepts.
