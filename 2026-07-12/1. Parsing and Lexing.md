I suggest that thinking about PEG as just another parser technology is too limiting. We already know how to build parsers. We already have lots of compilers - we don't need more compilers.

Lexing plus parsing is just sophisticated string matching. That's the easiest part of building compilers.

Does PEG bring something new to the table? We should want to build sophisticated string matchers that don't need separate lexers. Does PEG provide that kind of ability? Could we build tools that are better than `grep` with PEG? Could we build a better kind of `perl`? My emacs has a "replace-regex" command - I wish it had "replace-peg-match" command.

Can we build even higher level syntaxes over top of the programming languages that we already have?
